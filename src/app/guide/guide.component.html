Display: Analogue (Analog) vs Digital Watches

To begin your hunt for the perfect watch, you need to first decide on the display. The top two display choices include analog and digital watches.

Analogue Watches:
A common display for watches, Analogue watches display time via hands on the dial and include the hour hand, the minute hand, and the second hand. These watches are more traditional and elegant compared to a digital watch. These timepieces offer varied designs and are highly durable, making them a great choice for all occasions.
Digital Watches:
These watches use an LCD screen to display time electronically. It is only available with quartz movement and displays time in minutes & hours while some digital watches also show seconds. Digital watches might not be as sophisticated or elegant as analog watches, but they are much more cost-effective. Some digital watches also offer additional features such as calendar, timer, etc. However, they are prone to wear & tear and not ideal for long-term use. Hybrid watches (a combination of the first two) and touchscreen watches (includes a touchscreen display) are a few of the other wristwatches for men.

Watch Movement: Quartz vs Mechanical

Next, you need to consider the watch movement, which is the mechanism that drives (or give life) to the watch. Generally, they are divided into two categories – quartz and mechanical.

Quartz:
This is the common type of mechanism used in watches and is known for its accuracy and durability. Compared to mechanical watches, quartz watches are more affordable & practical and can be distinguished from the ‘jump’ the second hand makes, when moving from one second to another.
Mechanical:
These watches have a rotor attached to the movement; run by mechanical parts. They can be either hand-wound (self–winding) or automatic (the rotor spins as your wrist moves). The motion of the second hand is smooth & sweeping compared to quartz watches. However, mechanical watches are more expensive and not as accurate as quartz watches.

Energy Source: Quartz or Solar or Smartwatch

When considering the different watch styles, you also need to check the energy source that powers the watch. Quartz watches are battery-operated while solar watches are powered by a solar cell that absorbs solar energy via photovoltaic solar cells to convert light into electrical energy. Smartwatches, in contrast, are powered by a rechargeable lithium-ion battery.

Design and Strap Material

If you are buying a watch for daily wear, stick to light, comfortable, and practical designs. Conventional and vintage designs are most suited for formal occasions as they have timeless appeal. Whereas, if you have an active lifestyle, buy functional watches with high durability.
Leather watch-band and stainless steel mesh straps are the most popular watch strap material used. High-quality strap material will ensure long-lasting use. When choosing a strap material, consider flexibility, high-strength, durability, comfort, sweat and water resistance. Apart from the above, other strap materials include mesh textured silicone band, ceramic watch-band, titanium watch-band, among others.

Comfort, Size and Budget

Your comfort level should be the top consideration when buying a watch. No matter how impressive and feature-rich the watch is, if doesn’t feel comfortable, it is not right for you.
